# üïµÔ∏è OSINT
OSINT, or Open Source Intelligence, refers to the process of collecting and analyzing publicly available information to produce actionable intelligence. This information can come from a variety of sources including media reports, internet publications, social media platforms, public government data, and academic journals. OSINT is widely used in fields such as national security, law enforcement, and business intelligence to gain insights without the need for covert or classified methods. Its primary advantage lies in its accessibility and legality, making it a valuable tool for various investigative and analytical purposes.
